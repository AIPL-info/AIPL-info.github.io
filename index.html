The International Workshop on AI for Personalized Learning (AIPL 2025)

Advancing the future of education through artificial intelligence and personalized learning approaches

Workshop: November 30, 2025 | Conference: December 1-3, 2025 | Hong Kong

Workshop Description

The International Workshop on AI for Personalized Learning (AIPL 2025) is dedicated to exploring the transformative impact of artificial intelligence on education, with a special focus on personalized learning. This workshop brings together researchers, educators, practitioners, and industry experts to discuss the latest advancements, practical applications, and future trends in AI-driven personalized education.

Key topics include intelligent tutoring systems, adaptive learning platforms, learner modeling, data-driven curriculum design, ethical considerations, and the integration of AI tools across diverse educational settings. By fostering interdisciplinary dialogue and sharing innovative research and real-world case studies, AIPL 2025 aims to advance the development of scalable, inclusive, and effective personalized learning solutions.

Participants are invited to contribute original research, case studies, and theoretical perspectives that highlight the opportunities and challenges of leveraging AI to tailor educational experiences to individual learners' needs, preferences, and abilities. Join us to shape the future of education through the power of artificial intelligence.

Call for Papers

We invite researchers, educators, practitioners, and industry experts to submit original papers to the International Workshop on AI for Personalized Learning (AIPL 2025). The workshop focuses on the latest advances, challenges, and future directions in AI-driven personalized learning, including but not limited to intelligent tutoring systems, adaptive learning platforms, learner modeling, data-driven curriculum design, ethical considerations, and the integration of AI tools in diverse educational settings.

Submission Guidelines

  • Submissions must present original research results, novel applications, or theoretical perspectives in technology-enhanced and personalized learning.
  • Papers should be formatted according to the Springer LNCS Authors Guidelines: Information for Authors.
  • Submission formats:
    • Full Paper: 12–15 pages (including references)
    • Short Paper: 6–11 pages (including references)
  • All submissions must be in PDF format and submitted via the Submission Portal.
  • The review process will be double-blind. Authors should ensure that manuscripts do not contain names, affiliations, or any information that may reveal their identity.

Publication

All accepted papers presented at the workshop will be published jointly with the post-conference proceedings in the Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) series.

Special Opportunities

Outstanding papers will be recommended for possible publication in a special issue of:
